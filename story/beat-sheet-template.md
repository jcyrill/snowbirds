# Beat Sheet - Snow Birds

Based on Blake Snyder's "Save the Cat" structure (15 beats), populated with story details.

---

## ACT 1

### 1. Opening Image (Page 1)
**Visual:** Zack and Kyle wakeboarding in Lake Tahoe, luxury boat, beautiful girls, partying—living the ultimate rich kid summer fantasy.  
**Mood:** High-energy, carefree entitlement.  
**What it shows about the world/character:** Privileged bros with no responsibilities, mastering the Whirlybird trick, ignoring real life.  

---

### 2. Theme Stated (Page 5)
**Who says it:** A party girl or elderly bystander.  
**The line/moment:** "You can't ride waves forever—eventually you gotta grow up and respect the tide."  
**What the story is really about:** Learning respect for elders and earning your way vs. entitlement.  

---

### 3. Setup (Pages 1-10)
**Protagonist's world:** Wealthy SF twins in Tahoe, partying, womanizing, dreaming of California Beach State.  
**Supporting characters introduced:** Parents (phone), party friends.  
**Protagonist's flaw/problem:** No responsibility, disrespect elders.  
**Stakes established:** College fund at risk if they screw up.  

**Key scenes in this section:**
- Scene 1: Wakeboarding montage  
- Scene 2: Party house night  

---

### 4. Catalyst/Inciting Incident (Page 12)
**The event that changes everything:** Crash into cat memorial luncheon, destroy urn/auction, video goes viral.  
**How protagonist reacts initially:** Laugh it off, then panic.  

---

### 5. Debate (Pages 12-25)
**Protagonist's resistance:** Downplay disaster, beg parents.  
**Why they don't want to change:** Love their lifestyle.  
**Internal conflict:** First taste of consequences.  

**Key scenes in this section:**
- Scene 4: Parents reveal costs  
- Scene 5: Ultimatum  

---

### 6. Break Into Two (Page 25)
**The decision:** Choose Florida over military.  
**Point of no return:** Arrive in community, grandparents leave.  
**Entering the "upside-down world":** From luxury to dump housing/jobs.  

---

## ACT 2A

### 7. B Story (Page 30)
**The relationship/subplot:** Romance with Daisy & Delia, nurses who teach respect/values.  
**Character introduced:** Daisy & Delia.  
**How this relates to theme:** They show genuine care for elders, contrast brothers' entitlement.  

---

### 8. Fun and Games (Pages 30-55)
**The "promise of the premise":** Disguised as old people training in senior community.  
**What the audience came to see:** Raunchy gags, disguise comedy, wakeboarding mishaps.  

**Major comedic set pieces:**
1. Disguise attempts (Scene 12)  
2. First training (Scene 13)  
3. Meeting girls (Scene 14)  
4. Raunchy discovery (Scene 15)  

**Character moments:**
- Helping seniors (Scene 16)  
- Training montage (Scene 17)  

---

### 9. Midpoint (Page 55)
**False victory or false defeat:** False victory - improving at Whirlybird.  
**Major revelation:** Kyle caught by Karen, forced encounter.  
**Stakes raised:** Secret at risk, must continue.  
**Time clock introduced/accelerated:** Miami contest approaching.  

---

## ACT 2B

### 10. Bad Guys Close In (Pages 55-75)
**External pressure:** Rivals Brand/Chad, developer's scheme.  
**Internal doubt:** Lies straining relationships.  
**Relationships fracture:** Girls suspicious.  

**Key scenes in this section:**
- Scene 21: Rivals arrive  
- Scene 22: Scheme revealed  
- Scene 23: Relationship complications  
- Scene 26: Rivalry intensifies  

---

### 11. All Is Lost (Page 75)
**The low point:** Full exposure at event.  
**Loss/failure:** Lose girls, access, hope.  
**Whiff of death (literal or metaphorical):** Community about to be destroyed.  

---

### 12. Dark Night of the Soul (Pages 75-85)
**Protagonist's despair:** Alone in dump, everything failed.  
**Questioning everything:** Doubt change possible.  
**Reflection on journey:** Realize they care now.  

---

## ACT 3

### 13. Break Into Three (Page 85)
**The revelation:** Seniors knew, want to help.  
**A + B = C (solution comes from combining lessons):** Disguise taught empathy + community support = win for all.  
**New approach:** Team up to compete and save community.  

---

### 14. Finale (Pages 85-110)
**The plan:** Enter Miami with help, win, sacrifice money.  
**Confrontation:** Vs. Brand/Chad at competition.  
**Resolution:** Pay debt, get scholarship, reconcile.  

**Key sequences:**
- Everyone knows/plan (Scenes 37-38)  
- Competition (Scenes 39-41)  
- Sacrifice/resolution (Scenes 43-47)  

**Character arc completed:** Entitled to empathetic.  

---

### 15. Final Image (Page 110)
**Visual:** Classroom with brothers studying, girls teaching, seniors mentoring.  
**Mood:** Hopeful, grown-up.  
**How it mirrors/inverts Opening Image:** From reckless partying to academic focus.  
**What we've learned:** Respect and growth trump entitlement.
