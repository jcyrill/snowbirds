# Questions to Answer Before Writing

## Critical Story Questions

### Act 1
- [ ] **What exactly do Zack & Kyle ruin in Tahoe?**
  - Must be expensive (college-fund level)
  - Must involve/disrespect elderly people
  - Must be comedic but serious enough for consequences
  - Options to explore:
    - Elderly charity gala/fundraiser
    - Someone's anniversary/memorial
    - Community center or memorial structure
    - Elderly wakeboarding attempt (they mock it, ruin it)

- [ ] **What's the boys' last name?**

- [ ] **Where in San Francisco are they from?**
  - Pac Heights? Marina District? 

- [ ] **How long is the summer timeline?**
  - Need to know how much time until Miami competition

### Act 2
- [ ] **Who are the two girls? Names, jobs, personalities?**
  - What's their connection to retirement community?
  - Why are young women there?
  - Options:
    - Lifeguards at community pool
    - Working summer jobs (cafeteria, activities coordinator)
    - Volunteers/community service
    - Grandchildren visiting for summer
  - What are their personalities?
  - How do they differ from girls the brothers usually chase?

- [ ] **Developer's name and sons' names?**
  - Need characterization for them
  - What's developer's specific scheme?
  - How are they drugging residents (if that's the plot)?

- [ ] **Corrupt admin character?**
  - Name, personality
  - How are they helping developer?
  - What's their motivation?

- [ ] **Grandparents' names and personalities?**
  - How long is their cruise?
  - What's their dynamic?
  - Are they cool/funny?

- [ ] **The "noisy neighbor" character?**
  - Name, personality
  - How does Kyle end up having to sleep with her?
  - How many times does this happen?
  - Is this running gag or one scene?

- [ ] **What's the "dump" apartment vs. grandparents' nice condo dynamic?**
  - Why are they supposed to live in dump?
  - Who owns it?
  - Why can't they just stay in grandparents' place?

### Act 3
- [ ] **How do they expose the developer scheme?**
  - What's the specific plan?
  - Do old people help?
  - Is there evidence to gather?

- [ ] **How does the prize money save the community?**
  - What specifically needs money?
  - How much is the prize?
  - Is it enough?

- [ ] **Do they compete in Miami or not?**
  - If yes: How do they get there if exposed?
  - If yes: How do they win but give up money?
  - Alternative: Competition happens at community instead?

- [ ] **How do they win back the girls?**
  - What's the specific gesture/proof?
  - Does one brother succeed first?

- [ ] **How do they end up paying for college?**
  - Parents forgive them and pay?
  - Community pools money?
  - Scholarships from character growth?
  - They get jobs?
  - They decide college isn't the answer?

- [ ] **What happens to developer and sons?**
  - Jail? Public humiliation?
  - What's the comeuppance?

### Wakeboarding Specifics
- [ ] **What's the "ultimate trick" they're working on?**
  - Needs a name
  - Needs to be visually impressive
  - Should require both brothers (teamwork)
  - Research: What tricks existed in 90s?

- [ ] **How good are they really?**
  - Amateur? Semi-pro? Just rich kids with toys?
  - Are developer's sons better?

- [ ] **What's the facility actually like?**
  - Private lake? Cable park? Boat only?
  - How realistic is "world-class facility" in retirement community?
  - Maybe it was built by wealthy founding resident who was professional?

### Comedy Specifics
- [ ] **What are the Villages-style details we're parodying?**
  - Golf carts everywhere
  - Garage door open = swinger signal?
  - Flamingos meaning something?
  - Activity schedule
  - Demographics
  - Rules and regulations

- [ ] **How bad are the disguises?**
  - Just wigs and makeup?
  - Old person suits?
  - Voice changing?
  - Smell?

- [ ] **What specific raunchy "old people" behavior do we show?**
  - Need to balance comedy with not being mean
  - Subvert expectations - they're MORE wild than young people
  - STD rates, partying, drinking, etc.
  - But keep them dignified overall

### Character Details
- [ ] **Why "Snow Birds" as title?**
  - Are grandparents snowbirds (winter in Florida)?
  - Is there a metaphor?
  - Or is it ironic (California kids in Florida)?

- [ ] **California Beach State - where does this fit?**
  - Is this their college they want to attend?
  - "Think Gronk at Arizona State" = party school
  - Is this important to plot?

### Tone Questions
- [ ] **How raunchy is too raunchy?**
  - Hard R-rating but where's the line?
  - Nudity? Simulated sex? How much?
  - Language?
  - Keep it fun, not gross

- [ ] **How much heart vs. comedy?**
  - Ratio of jokes to genuine moments?
  - When does tone shift?
  - How schmaltzy is the redemption?

### Practical Questions
- [ ] **Time period: Early, mid, or late 90s?**
  - Affects tech, music, fashion, references
  - Suggestion: 1997-1998
    - Peak wakeboarding popularity
    - Tech not too primitive but no smartphones
    - Best music/pop culture options
    - Post-grunge, pre-boy band peak

- [ ] **How long is grandparents' cruise?**
  - Affects timeline
  - When do they come back?
  - Do they come back before ending?

- [ ] **What's the budget level for filming?**
  - Affects locations, stunts, cast size
  - Is this indie or studio?
  - Practical wakeboarding vs. CGI?

### Research Needed
- [ ] Actual Villages community rules/culture
- [ ] Wakeboarding tricks and terminology (1990s era)
- [ ] Florida retirement communities (architecture, lifestyle)
- [ ] 1997-1998 specific pop culture
- [ ] Real estate development schemes against seniors
- [ ] Elder abuse/fraud plots for authenticity
- [ ] Wakeboarding competition structures
- [ ] Cost of college in 1990s (for prize money amount)

## Questions to Ask User

Should prioritize getting answers to:
1. What do they ruin in Act 1? (crucial for plot)
2. Exact year in 90s? (affects everything)
3. Who are the love interest girls? (need for character development)
4. How does Act 3 resolve? (need ending before writing)
5. What's the ultimate trick? (visual anchor of film)
